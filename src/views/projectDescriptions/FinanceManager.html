Finance Manager WebApp

A fullstack web app designed to calculate your net financial standing across all your accounts.

Project Overview

This finance manager project was born out of being annoyed at the number of different financial accounts I had to manually log into and check balances of. I wanted to create a centralized platform where I could load all assets balances and other financial data so I could get a better picture of what my current financial standing was, and where I should try to aim next.

The webapp allows you to simply add, subtract, edit, and delete assets, equities, or cash you hold in each financial account and it will automatically consolidate everything into one easy UI that depicts your real time net worth and other useful financial metrics such as stock data and investment performance over time.

It works on a classic full stack architecture with a React frontend, and then the FastAPI backend that stores all user data into SQLite databases for persistent local storage. The backend also communicates with third party financial APIs to pull real time stock data to give users up to date information on their investments through libraries like yfinance. This architecture also allows for future mobile app expansion as the backend can easily service multiple frontends.

Challenges

One of the main challenges was to figure out what exactly I wanted to track. There are so many different financial measurements to track and so many of them overlap that I needed to really think about and track what was relevant to a net worth calculator dashboard.

So to start I began with the things that build up my own net worth, things like my stock portfolios, my 401k, my ROTH accounts, my cash accounts, and my US Treasuries. All of these combined make up my net worth, so I focused on building out features to track these first before expanding to other financial metrics.

As such you will find that things like price data and history of equities that exist in my portfolio are tracked, but things like debt tracking or credit score tracking are not initially implemented as they do not directly affect net worth.

Additionally, I plan to incorporate features for tracking expenses and income and possibly using AI summarization and suggestions in future updates to provide a more comprehensive financial overview.
